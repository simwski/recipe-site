## Background
I (Simone) was feeling inspired by watching other people eat Taiwanese sesame noodles on TikTok. But I was concerned the sesame flavour would trigger taste fatigue after the first few bites. Also, the lack of protein in most recipes concerned me. Tofu came up as a suggestion in my research, but A: I’ve never been good at cooking tofu, and B: I can picture how disappointed Basim would be watching me prepare it. So I headed to the Asian supermarket for inspo and worked it out. This meal is yummm.

## Ingredients

- Fresh wheat soba noodles (don’t bother if they’re not fresh!!!)
- 2 tablespoons Tahini (I think I tasted it and added more tho)
- 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons dark soy sauce
- 1⁄2 teaspoon Chinese black vinegar (or rice vinegar)
- 1 tablespoon Miso paste
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 2 teaspoons brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon chili oil (optional)
- Green spring onion
- 3 teaspoons of minced garlic
- 400g Shiitake mushroom 
- 1⁄3 cup (80 g) hot water (from cooking noodles)
- 1 pinch salt, to taste
- Mince beef
- Bok choy


## Method

### Veggies (feel free to do this way in advance if you want)

1. Prep your bok choy by chopping off the nasty bits
2. Wash to get away all the dirties
3. Cover it in salt and let it sit. This will help soften it and make it tastier to use.
4. Before combining into meal, wash off the excess salt.
5. Cut off the white parts of spring onion so they’re long (not tiny pieces).
6. Cut the green parts into tiny pieces.

### Tahini sauce
1. Combine Tahini, light soy, dark soy, Chinese black vinegar, miso paste, sesame oil, & brown sugar in a cup.
2. Give it a mix and a taste.
3. If you want strong nutty, sesame flavours, add more tahini.

### Noodles
1. Follow packet instructions and cook the noodles
2. When completed, <strong> DO NOT </strong> tip out the noodle water!! You will use this later!!!
3. Take noodles out of the water to strain and avoid them getting mushy. Run cold water over them too to prevent them cooking further.

### Main meal
1. Heat up a pan. Make sure the pan is big enough for all of the ingredients by the end. You could also use a pot.
2. Wait until the pan is actually hot, then put the mince on.
3. Spread the mince so it covers the surface area of the pan, and sprinkle a bit of salt and pepper.
4. Now stop touching the mince! Let it do its thing. First, all the water will come out of it. We want to keep cooking it until that moisture evaporates  and cooks off. Keep an eye on it - once the water goes, the cook will progress quickly.
5. When it’s looking nice and brown, and at about 90% complete, take the mince out of the pan and into a separate bowl.
6. Hopefully there are juices left behind from the mince. If not, add a bit of oil. Turn the heat down.
7. Smack the white parts of the green onion a bit then put them in the pan for about a minute or two.
8. Add the garlic and cook it for a little.
9. Add chopped shiitake mushrooms, cook until they begin to brown.
10. Add 1/3 of hot water from the noodles. Scrape the bottom of the pan to get all that browned goodness from the mince.
11. Bring it to boil, add the mince and the bok choy, then let it simmer for about 10 mins.
12. Take the bok choy out and put it onto a cute side plate.
13. Start adding the tahini sauce, but only put a little bit in each time and taste until you’re happy with it. One time I overdid it by pouring a whole sauce inside without tasting and the whole meal turned out horrible.
- It should taste a little like Vegemite towards the end of the flavour, but don’t panic.
- It’s weird at first but that is just the Tahini.
- Once you add the noodles, that weirdness goes away.
14. Let it simmer a little longer, and if you’re happy with it, turn off the heat.
15. Stir in the noodles.
16. Serve with a sprinkle of green onions and sesame seeds on top
